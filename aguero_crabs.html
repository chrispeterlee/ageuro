Peak Year
2017 was the golden age — 28,000 messages, nearly triple the 2016 founding. February alone saw 4,140 messages in a single month. The group had found its voice and absolutely would not stop using it.
The Long Slide
From the 2017 peak, activity roughly halved by 2022. Life happened. People moved abroad, got jobs, had kids. The chat didn't die — it just aged into something quieter and (marginally) more considered.
Lockdown Spike
2020 bucked the trend slightly. Turns out when nobody can go anywhere, the WhatsApp group becomes a lifeline. October 31, 2020 — 619 messages watching rugby at midnight — is the single busiest day in the chat's history.
The Comeback
2024 and 2025 show a modest revival. Up from the 2022–23 trough. Perhaps friendship is, in fact, a renewable resource. Or there was just more rugby on.

The Birth
Jamie Martin Grace created a group called "USA Alpha Males" and immediately added everyone. Chris Scollard changed the icon within minutes. The first actual item of business, per Chris Lee: is anyone getting a few cold beers later?

Banna Beach
The group's first big social event. Banna Beach, Kerry — September 2016 — a lads' weekend away. Rob McMahon only found out he wasn't being sent to Saudi Arabia in time to join. Costumes were discussed. Eoin Gray was chasing €52.50 from people. The group had found its first tradition.

2017
27,989 messages · The Golden Year
Peak Everything
The group's single biggest year by a mile. February alone saw over 4,140 messages. January 20 (364 messages) was one of the top 10 busiest days ever. The lads were in their prime.
The Beginning of the End (for some)
Gary Foxton sends just 31 messages all year — down from 173 in 2016. He's still technically present, but he's already fading. Tim White-Flynn joins mid-year, fires off 113 messages, then starts his long drift toward silence.
On the Lions Tour
The British & Irish Lions vs NZ tour generated some of the group's most intense activity. June 8 and 13 both cracked 350+ messages. The group had opinions. Many of them.
"Lions need to use that flat ball more often."
— Matty Morrow, definitive analyst

October 2017. Mark O'Caglahan floated Dungeons & Dragons. The group countered with Secret Hitler. Luke Madden declared it "so goooood". Lorcan Clarke: "the king of all games". Chris Scollard: "Does it tick all the Reich boxes?" Chris Lee subsequently ordered it from the US via AddressPal.

Gary Foxton sends his final 20 messages. His last ever message in the Aguero Crabs is on 18 August 2019. We don't know what it was about. We do know he called himself Fat Tony once, and that's enough.

Luke Madden drops from 3,489 messages in 2017 to just 476 in 2019. The biggest fall from grace in the group. To be fair, he was still 9th overall. He came back a bit in later years.

March 2, 2019. Chris Lee floated pints. Gary Foxton confirmed he'd be "floating about town from 8" with Scully and Ross. Luke had a dinner until 8:30. Rob needed to check his plans. The group converged. It remains one of the last documented nights out with the full complement before the numbers started thinning.

In Memoriam

Every group has them. The ones who were there, then weren't. This is not a condemnation — life happens, group chats drift, phones get lost. But the data is the data.

A founding member. 178 messages in 2016. Held on through 2019 (29 messages), 2020 (42), 2021 (15). Then five in 2022 and gone. His most used word in later years was "lads" — always reaching out, less and less often.

Chris Scollard — who changed the group icon in the first five minutes — went from 3,243 in 2017 to 299 in 2025, including one 38-day gap of silence. The co-founder, fading quietly.
